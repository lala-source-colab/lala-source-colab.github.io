Day 15
You are here. Acute phase done. Moderate loading begins.

Pain rule: Mild tightness is normal. Sharp, searing, or worsening pain = stop and rest 2 weeks before retrying.

✅ Phase 1 — Days 0–14 — Complete

What You Already Did Right

✓ Protected the Repair

  • Lifting kept under 15 lbs
  • Walking only for cardio
  • No direct core work
  • Incisions kept clean and dry

✓ Gentle Activation

  • Diaphragmatic breathing
  • Daily walks, increasing distance
  • Gentle hip and hamstring stretches
  • No sit-ups, crunches, or planks

Mesh integration is underway. You are cleared to move into Phase 2.

🚴 Phase 2 — Days 15–45 — Cardio

Cardio: Start This Week

🚴

Stationary Bike

Start now. Low resistance, 15–20 min. First green-lit cardio after repair.

🏃

Elliptical

Add after bike feels comfortable. Low resistance. Zero impact on repair site.

🏊

Swimming

Wait until incisions fully closed & dry. Typically weeks 3–4 post-op.

Progression Rule

Increase duration first, then resistance. Never both in the same week.

Running — Not Yet

Pool jogging begins weeks 5–6. Outdoor running: weeks 7+.

💪 Phase 2 — Days 15–45 — Strength

Strength: Body Weight & Light Resistance

✅ Do These Now

  • Mini-squats (partial range, body weight)
  • Forward & reverse lunges
  • Heel raises (standing)
  • Step-downs off a low step
  • Lateral mini-band walks
  • Sidelying hip abduction (clamshells)
  • Light leg press machine

🚫 Avoid Until Week 6+

  • Sit-ups & crunches
  • Full planks
  • Overhead pressing
  • Twisting under load
  • Explosive movements
  • Lifting over 25 lbs
📏

Lifting limit: up to 25 lbs. Stop if you feel pulling, sharp pain, or a bulge at the repair site.

Days 15–45 · 10 reps, 1–2 sets daily

Core Activation: Safe Now

Pelvic Tilt

Lie on back. Tighten abdomen, tilt hips back, pull belly to floor. Hold 5 sec.

Diaphragmatic Breathing

Slow deep breath 4x/day. Exhale fully, then fill lungs completely.

Core Tightening

Standing. Pull belly button to spine, hold 5 sec. Practice throughout the day.

Standing Knee Raise

Hold chair for balance. Alternate knee raises slowly. 10 each side.

Heel Taps (Lying)

Knees to 90°. Alternate tapping each heel to floor, core braced throughout.

Cat / Cow

Hands and knees. Gently round then extend spine. Slow, controlled. Great for stiffness.

Days 15–45 · Pain-Free Only

Stretching, Mobility & Pain Relief

✅ Safe Stretches

  • Hip flexor — kneeling lunge, upright torso
  • Hamstring — seated or supine, gentle
  • Quad — standing, hold ankle
  • Adductor — seated butterfly or side lunge
  • Hip circles — standing range of motion

🚫 Avoid

  • Deep abdominal stretching
  • Aggressive trunk rotation
  • Any stretch creating pulling at incision

Stiffness & pain: Confirm Tylenol vs Advil with Dr. Passarelli. Ice 15–20 min on sore areas. Gentle movement beats rest alone.

🔥 Phase 3 — Weeks 5–6

Core Engagement & Light Jogging

Core Progression

  • Bridging → progress to single-leg bridge
  • Bird dog — opposite arm/leg from back
  • Quadruped — 4-point → 3-point → 2-point
  • Plank — start on knees, 5-sec holds only
  • Gentle curl-ups after surgeon OK at wk 6

Cardio & Strength Upgrades

  • Pool jogging (preferred) or easy treadmill jog
  • Light upper body — machines only, no overhead
  • Increase resistance on bike and elliptical
  • Farmers carry — 5 lb each hand

6-week milestone: Mesh is ~85% healed. Most lifting restrictions lift. Confirm with Dr. Passarelli before advancing to Phase 4.

🏆 Phase 4 — Weeks 7–12

Return to Triathlon Training

🏃

Running

Run/walk intervals. Increase run time weekly. Build mileage slowly.

🏊

Swimming

Full lap sessions. Build distance progressively. Open water after week 8.

🚴

Cycling

Road/tri bike at wk 6–8. Ease into aero position — it loads the core.

Strength Training

Return at ~50% prior weights. Add 5–10% per week. No HIIT/plyos until wk 8–10.

Week 12 Goal

Full return to half-triathlon training. All restrictions lifted.

Know When to Stop

Warning Signs — Call Dr. Passarelli

New or worsening bulge

At the umbilical repair site. Could indicate hernia recurrence.

Sudden sharp abdominal pain

Different from normal tightness. Sharp or stabbing. Stop all activity immediately.

Fever, redness, or discharge

At the incision site. Signs of possible infection requiring prompt attention.

Nausea + abdominal pain together

Possible bowel obstruction. Seek emergency care immediately.

Normal vs concerning: Tightness, stiffness, and mild soreness are expected for weeks. Sharp, sudden, or worsening = call the office.
